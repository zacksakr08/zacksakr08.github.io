My Essay

A personal reflection on place, memory, and what it means to call New York home.

By Zack Sakr

AP Language and Composition / NEST+M — 2024

Beyond the Stereotypes of NYC

The big city has always been perceived as an icon of the American dream, where one can thrive and reach their true potential. The city is usually seen by someone who doesn’t live in the City through one lens. This lens is that it will be hard to accomplish that dream if you are not a rich white person. The idea is that if you don’t fit specific criteria, you won't be able to belong in New York City. This idea comes from the minority of rich people in the city that the media publicize to show how great the Big Apple is and how the City is for a select few.

Someone might think if they walked down the streets of New York, they would see mostly white people in suits on their way to a steakhouse to talk about how NASDAQ is down three percent from last quarter. This idea of the City can be intimidating, especially when you look up and see a forest of obelisks stretching out as far as the eye can see, filled with lawyers, business owners, start-ups, hedge funds, and famous influencers. This intimidation of the city could cause one to be nervous to consider even moving here because one doesn't fit the criteria of the “perfect New Yorker.” While everyone goes and chases this perfect ideology of what it means to be a New Yorker, I sit back and think about how that version of an ideal New Yorker is so far from the truth. People perceive New York this way because of the mass media and how only the very rich and famous get publicized, which causes people to believe in this fake New York.

New York is not a place just swarmed with the wealthy, celebrities, and influencers; on the contrary, New York is filled with hustlers and people who need to work two jobs just to pay rent. New York is filled with culture and experiences with people who have to struggle but love the struggle because they are aiming for the top one percent and thrive to make it to the top. New York is for everyone willing to do the work and put in the effort. New York reminds me of an AP class. It is definitely possible to do well in it, but it definitely will take time and energy to succeed, and if you don't put in the effort you will fall behind. New York is similar in the sense that if you don't keep hustling and don't try to keep up with the rest of the city, you will fall behind and feel like you're not “rich” enough or not “famous” enough for New York when in reality it is because you don't have the New Yorker mentality.

Being a New Yorker is a mentality. It doesn't come from your wealth, background, or your education. It comes from how much you want that dream, the dream that you dreamt when you were a kid and stared at the ceiling on those sleepless nights, thinking about how you want to move to that penthouse on Central Park South while sipping on wine with the love of your life and you can finally exhale and say “I did it.” This mentality can get any man, woman, race, or anybody to the top of the social hierarchy in this beautiful city.

This is the best city in the world. There is no other city with a unique blend of culture, wealth, personality, and history like New York. The city doesn't have a set of rules you need to follow to succeed. You just need perseverance and dedication to whatever you're doing, which could be any number of things. It could be a startup tech company or a clothing brand. You just need to love and keep at it, and with experience and the amount of brilliant people that are in this city, you will finally feel like you fit into the big city.

These truths about the city are evident in many different parts of the city; my neighborhood is a perfect example of the idea that New York is just filled with affluent business owners, and it's just one group of people in the city. The Financial District in downtown Manhattan is usually seen as a place just filled with people with an abundance of money and work on Wall Street, with degrees from the best schools and have come from a line of family members who come from money and did similar work. This is far from the truth, and in fact, the Financial District does a great job of representing New York as a misinterpreted city, and these perceptions are filled with false truths.

The Financial District feels like a suburban town where everyone knows each other and is a warm welcoming community. It isn't just some financial, white person, angry filled place. Just like most of the city and how the city is one large community and how we all can relate with each other in some way shape or form. New York is the best city in the world. You can go down one block, see one country and go down another and see a different one. New York will keep being deceived for what it truly is, but it doesn't matter as long as it keeps its truths.

The Financial District represents the city as a whole so well because there are many misconceptions about it that are just not true. Although there are definitely wealthy people who live here, there is a lot of diversity and culture throughout. There are many different authentic restaurants and many small businesses are starting up and trying to rise to the top to reach their end goal. These different cultures and ethnicities help shape what the Financial District is and give it character and personality.
